DIY Mickey Mouse Cake

Ingredients

  • For the cake

    1. 2 cups all-purpose flour, sifted (because even Mickey wouldn't tolerate lumpy flour)
    2. 1 1/2 cups granulated sugar, for sweetness that'll make you shout "Oh, boy!"
    3. 3/4 cup unsweetened cocoa powder, for a rich, dark chocolate flavor as deep as Mickey's voice
    4. teaspoon baking soda, to give the cake a lift, just like Mickey's positive attitude
    5. 1/2 teaspoon salt, a pinch of magic to balance the sweetness
    6. 1 cup buttermilk, for a moist and tender crumb like Mickey's big, soft ears
    7. 1/2 cup vegetable oil, to keep things smooth like Mickey's dance moves
    8. 2 large eggs, for binding and richness, because Mickey's cake deserves the best
    9. 1 teaspoon vanilla extract, a touch of magic to make the cake sing like a chorus of Mickeys

  • For the frosting

    1. 1 cup (2 sticks) unsalted butter, softened to the texture of Mickey's warm smile
    2. 3 cups powdered sugar, for sweetness that'll have you whistling "Heigh-Ho"
    3. 1/2 cup unsweetened cocoa powder, for a chocolatey depth that rivals Mickey's mysterious past
    4. 1/4 cup milk, for a smooth and creamy consistency like Mickey's velvety gloves
    5. 1 teaspoon vanilla extract, a sprinkle of pixie dust to make the frosting taste like a trip to Disneyland

  • For the decoration

    1. Black and white candy melts, for crafting Mickey's iconic features like a skilled Imagineer
    2. Red candy melts, for a splash of color that'll make your cake as cheerful as Mickey's laugh
    3. Round chocolate wafers, for Mickey's iconic ears, because who wants a Mickey Mouse cake without the ears?
    4. Sprinkles, because every Mickey Mouse cake deserves a sprinkle of magic (and because sprinkles are just plain fun)

Instructions

  1. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C) and grease two 9-inch round cake pans. Remember, a well-greased pan is a happy pan, and a happy pan makes a happy cake, just like a happy Mickey makes a happy world.
  2. In a large bowl, whisk together the flour, sugar, cocoa powder, baking soda, and salt. Imagine you're conducting an orchestra of dry ingredients, each playing its part in the symphony of deliciousness.
  3. In a separate bowl, whisk together the buttermilk, vegetable oil, eggs, and vanilla extract. This is the wet ingredient band, and they're ready to join the dry ingredient orchestra for a grand finale.
  4. Gradually add the wet ingredients to the dry ingredients, mixing until just combined. Don't overmix! You want a smooth and even batter, not a tough and overworked one. Remember, even Mickey takes breaks.
  5. Divide the batter evenly between the prepared cake pans and bake for 30-35 minutes, or until a toothpick inserted into the center comes out clean. While the cakes bake, imagine them transforming into Mickey

Enjoy your cake!
